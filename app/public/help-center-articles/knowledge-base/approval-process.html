What Is the Approval Process for an Apartment?

The apartment approval process is similar to taking out a loan‚Äîyou must prove you're financially responsible.

The rental application approval process involves several steps that landlords use to evaluate whether you'll be a reliable tenant.

The Application Process Step-by-Step

Step 1: Submit Application

You complete and submit a rental application including:

  • Personal information (name, SSN, DOB)
  • Employment and income details
  • Rental history
  • References
  • Emergency contacts

Step 2: Pay Application Fee

Non-refundable fee ($30-$75) to cover screening costs.

Step 3: Credit Check

Landlord reviews your:

  • Credit score (typically want 620+)
  • Payment history
  • Outstanding debts
  • Collections or bankruptcies

Step 4: Background Check

Criminal history is reviewed (varies by state what can be considered).

Step 5: Income Verification

Landlord confirms you earn enough (usually 2.5-3x monthly rent):

  • Pay stubs
  • Tax returns
  • Bank statements
  • Employment verification letter

Step 6: Reference Checks

Previous landlords are contacted to verify:

  • Rental payment history
  • Property care
  • Lease violations
  • Reason for leaving

Step 7: Decision

Landlord approves, denies, or requests additional information.

What Landlords Look For

  • Income: 2.5-3x monthly rent
  • Credit score: 620+ preferred
  • Rental history: No evictions
  • Employment: Stable job history
  • References: Positive landlord feedback

Common Reasons for Denial

  • Insufficient income
  • Poor credit history
  • Previous evictions
  • Criminal record
  • Negative landlord references
  • Incomplete application
  • False information

If You're Denied

Landlords must provide written notice explaining the reason for denial. You have the right to dispute inaccurate information.
